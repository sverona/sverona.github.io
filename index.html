How is this website designed?

June 8, 2019 — 9:37am

Since my personal website is (logically) a model for how I approach web design in general, I ought to write up a document detailing the decisions I've made. Here it is.

Generally

I consider the unnecessary use of JavaScript to be a cardinal sin.

I've adhered as far as my taste will allow to the principles of brutalist design, particularly the dictum:

...start with left-aligned black text on a white background, and [...] apply styling only to solve a specific problem.

The specific problems that my styling choices solve are mostly described here. Most everything not covered there is for either the site header or my personal sensibilities.

My commented stylesheet is here, if you'd like to confirm this.

Typesetting

I nicked the typeface, Source Serif Pro, from Gwern. In OTF format, the four fonts this site uses (Regular and Semibold, plus italics) collectively weigh in at 588 kB.

Colors

A simple site like this doesn't need more than one.

1729 is the smallest integer that is the sum of two positive perfect cubes in more than one way. Coincidentally, #f1729f is a very pretty pale pink, so I wanted to use it as my site's accent color. It's the color of hover links and of the drop-shadow at top left of this page, next to my name.

Links are set in #f23074, which is the same hue with the saturation cranked up for readability.
